Our story

From one building, a promise to a community.

Valmartin Healthcare opened its doors in 2021 with a simple conviction: that quality healthcare should not depend on where you live or what you can afford. From a single building in Saker, Limbe, we set out to be a clinic the community could trust at any hour.

What began as a small clinical practice has grown into a multidisciplinary team of doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and community health workers. We deliver more than 10 health services, run regular medical outreaches, host Mama and Baby Days for new mothers, and stay open 24 hours a day, every day of the year.

In April 2024, Valmartin was formally registered as a non-profit healthcare association under the laws of Cameroon, regulated by Article 7 of Law N° 90/053 of 19/12/90. That registration codified what our patients already knew: we are here to serve, not to extract.

Mission and vision

What we stand for, in plain words.

These statements guide every decision we make, from how we welcome patients at the door to how we structure our outreach in surrounding communities.

Our Mission

To deliver compassionate, quality healthcare that no one is turned away from.

We provide accessible, around-the-clock medical, maternal, and pharmaceutical care to the people of Limbe and surrounding communities, with particular attention to those whom the formal health system too often leaves behind.

Our Vision

A Cameroon where access to quality healthcare is a settled fact, not a privilege.

We see Valmartin growing into a recognised community health institution and a credible partner for government, donors, and research bodies working to close the access gap in Cameroon's most underserved health districts.

Our objectives

Three commitments, codified in law.

These are the official objectives Valmartin Healthcare Association is registered to pursue, as filed with the Senior Divisional Officer for Fako.

01

Facilitate access to healthcare

Facilitate access to health facilities for everyone by intervening in emergencies and transporting patients to the nearest health facility through ambulance services.

02

Advance research and surveillance

Enhance research on diseases and outbreaks, educate communities on preventive measures through seminars and health campaigns, and conduct continuous community sensitisation, epidemic surveillance, and reporting.

03

Investigate barriers to care

Investigate the structural and individual barriers that prevent communities from accessing quality healthcare, and design responses that address those barriers at the source.

Want to partner with us? We'd love to talk.

We welcome partnerships with NGOs, donors, research bodies, and government partners who share our commitment to health equity in Cameroon.
